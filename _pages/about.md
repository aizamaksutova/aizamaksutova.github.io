I am a Master's student in Robotics at **Johns Hopkins University**, where I am a member of the
[Unberath Lab](https://www.cs.jhu.edu/~unberath/) advised by [Prof. Mathias Unberath](https://engineering.jhu.edu/faculty/mathias-unberath/).
My research sits at the intersection of **surgical perception**, **computer vision**, and **geometric deep learning**,
with a focus on enabling intraoperative AI systems that understand the physical world of the operating room.

My core interest is in building models that reason about the spatial and physical properties of soft tissue — capturing how
tissues deform, how surgical tools interact with anatomical structures, and how scene geometry encodes
action-relevant affordances. Concretely, my work addresses (i) **dense affordance prediction** for tool-tissue interaction,
enabling robots and AI assistants to anticipate where and how instruments should engage tissue, and
(ii) **generative world models** for the operating room, combining digital twin representations with
video diffusion to synthesize controllable, photorealistic surgical scenes for data augmentation and simulation.

I am broadly interested in the following research directions:

- **Surgical scene understanding** — 3D reconstruction, semantic segmentation, and physical reasoning in endoscopic video
- **Affordance learning** — action-conditioned dense prediction for deformable, non-rigid environments
- **Generative models for medical data** — controllable video and image synthesis for surgical training and simulation
- **Foundation models for surgery** — adapting large vision-language models to the endoscopic domain

Prior to my current role, I gained industry experience as a Machine Learning Engineer/Intern,
where I developed and deployed deep learning models in production settings.
I also serve as a **Teaching Assistant** in the Department of Computer Science at JHU,
supporting graduate-level coursework in computer integrated surgery and robotics.

I am actively seeking **PhD positions starting Fall 2026** in robotics, computer vision, and medical AI.
If you are interested in my work or potential collaboration, please reach out.
